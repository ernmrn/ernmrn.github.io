Carbon Emissions by State

Which states have cut down on their carbon footprints over the past decade?

By Erin Moran

According to the Environmental Protection Agency, the United States has the second largest carbon footprint in the world, only after China. However, each state has different environmental challenges, levels of greenhouse gas emissions and strategies for overcoming those problems.

In fact, carbon emisions in many states have gone done in the past 15 years. From 2000 to 2013, CO2 emissions fell in 37 states and only rose in 13 states. However, in one year from 2012 to 2013, only 16 states saw a decrease in emissions, while 34 experienced an increase.

The largest percentage decrease per capita in CO2 emissions was in Maine at 6 million metric tons, or 27 percent of the state's total CO2 emissions. The largest absolute decline was 52 million metric tons, or 25 percent of the total CO2 emissions, in New York. This decrease was largely in part due to energy efficiency initiatives in New York City, like OneNYC Green Buildings & Energy Efficiency and Reforming the Energy Vision, or REV, Governor Andrew Cuomo's state energy strategy.

The state with both the greatest per capita and absolute increase was Nebraska, with an increase of 11 million metric tons, or 28 percent.
